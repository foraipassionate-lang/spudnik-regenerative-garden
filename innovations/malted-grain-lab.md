## The Problem

Traditional Korean Natural Farming LAB production requires:
1. Rinsing rice to capture starch water
2. Waiting 5-7 days for wild LAB to colonize
3. Feeding to milk to select LAB strains
4. Total time: 2-3 weeks minimum

This works, but requires purchasing rice and significant patience.

---

## The Hypothesis

**Malted barley grains from breweries contain pre-converted sugars** (the malting process converts starches to fermentable sugars). This should provide an even better food source for LAB than rice starch.

Additionally, brewery grains already have:
- Active yeast populations
- Residual enzymes (amylases, proteases)
- Porous structure for microbial colonization

---

## The Process

### Materials
- 500g expired malted brewery grains (often free from local breweries)
- 1L warm water
- 500ml whole milk
- Optional: 50ml molasses for storage

### Method

1. **Steep grains** in warm water (30-40Â°C) for 4-6 hours
2. **Strain** and collect the liquid (discard or compost grains)
3. **Wait** 3-5 days (faster than rice wash due to pre-converted sugars)
4. **Add to milk** at 1:10 ratio
5. **Wait** for separation into 3 layers (3-5 days)
6. **Harvest** middle serum layer
7. **Preserve** with equal parts molasses, or refrigerate

### Timeline Comparison

| Method | Starch Stage | Milk Stage | Total |
|--------|--------------|------------|-------|
| Traditional (rice) | 5-7 days | 5-7 days | 10-14 days |
| **Malted grain** | **3-5 days** | **3-5 days** | **6-10 days** |

## Observations

### What Worked
- Faster fermentation initiation (sugars immediately available)
- Strong fermentation smell within 24-48 hours
- Milk stage separation occurred normally
- Final serum has characteristic yogurt-like smell

## Applications

The resulting LAB serum can be used identically to traditional LAB:
- **Foliar spray:** 1:1000 dilution
- **Soil drench:** 1:500 dilution
- **Bokashi bran inoculation:** undiluted
- **Compost activator:** 1:500 dilution
